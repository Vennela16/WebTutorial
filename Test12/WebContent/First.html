Web Content Mining

Group 1(Raymond Muller, Vennela Vysyaraju, and Parker Gray)

Web Content Mining is the extraction and analysis of "useful" data contained in a webpage. What is considered "useful" depends on the application.There are two prevailing points of view in web content mining: the information retrieval view (IR) and Database View (DB).We will focus on the Information Retrieval aspect of Web Content Mining.The Information Retrieval view focuses on categorizing webpages by looking at the text of a webpage and using statistics and machine learning to determine the information of a page based on the words, phrases, and concepts contained therein. IR extracts rules and patterns in order to categorize the page.
